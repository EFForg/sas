We're standing against mass spying

Dear Mr. President:

As citizens of the Internet, we believe that mass surveillance by the NSA and its global partners infringes on our civil liberties, runs contrary to democratic principles, and chills free expression.

We’re calling on you to take immediate steps to end the mass spying. Specifically, we urge you to issue an executive order that will:

  1. Stop the mass collection of telephone records and Internet communications of hundreds of millions of people who are not suspected of a crime;

  2. Rescind executive order 12333, which the government cites as giving them a right to hack into the overseas data center links of US companies.

In addition, we call on you to provide a full public accounting of the intelligence community’s warrantless, mass surveillance practices.

We recognize that Congress has an important role to play in passing legislation to ensure no future programs of mass surveillance can exist. However, you are not obligated to wait for Congress to act. We urge you to use your executive powers to end mass surveillance immediately and embrace new levels of transparency around America’s spying apparatus. Only through transparency can real reform be enacted.

Surveillance should be targeted in nature and authorized by an independent court, not sweeping in the records of hundreds of millions of people with little discrimination. Once you have reined in the NSA spying programs, join us in asking Congress to enact reform to ensure these programs are permanently outlawed.

Sincerely,
